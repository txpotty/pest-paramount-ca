Do You Need Squirrel Removal in Paramount? Signs to Watch For

It's not always obvious when you have a squirrel problem, especially if they are nesting in less-visited areas like your attic. However, squirrels leave behind several telltale signs that indicate their presence. Recognizing these signs early can prevent more extensive damage and a larger infestation. If you notice any of the following, it's a good idea to contact Paramount Pest Pros for a professional squirrel removal service in Paramount, CA.

Scratching and Scurrying Sounds

One of the most common indicators of squirrels in your home is the sounds they make, especially in the early morning and late afternoon. Squirrels are most active during these times while foraging for food. If you hear scratching, scurrying, or chattering noises coming from your attic, walls, or chimney, there's a high probability you have unwanted squirrel guests. These sounds are often quite distinct and can be unsettling.

Visible Damage to Your Property

Squirrels are persistent chewers, and they will gnaw on almost anything to gain access to shelter or food, or simply to wear down their continuously growing teeth. Look for visible damage on the exterior of your home, such as chewed or gnawed areas on rooflines, soffits, fascia boards, siding, and vents. They might also tear apart insulation around pipes or in the attic. This damage can compromise the structural integrity of your home and lead to other issues like water leaks.

Chewed Wires and Pipes

Perhaps one of the most dangerous problems associated with squirrels is their tendency to chew on electrical wires. This can create a significant fire hazard. If you notice flickering lights, power outages, or even a burning smell, it could be a sign of squirrels chewing on wiring in your attic or walls. They may also chew on plumbing pipes, leading to leaks and water damage. Addressing this immediately is crucial for your safety.

Droppings and Urine

Squirrels will leave their waste products wherever they are nesting or traveling within your property. Finding squirrel droppings in your attic, garage, or other areas is a clear sign of an infestation. Squirrel droppings are typically small, pellet-shaped, and can vary in color depending on their diet. The accumulation of droppings and urine not only creates an unpleasant odor but also poses health risks due to potential pathogens.

Nesting Materials

Squirrels build nests using a variety of materials, including leaves, twigs, insulation, and shredded paper. If you find piles of these materials in sheltered areas of your home, such as your attic, chimney, or wall cavities, it's a strong indication of a squirrel nest. The presence of a nest suggests that squirrels are actively breeding or residing on your property, and removal is necessary to prevent further multiplication of the problem.

Sightings of Squirrels Entering or Leaving

Seeing squirrels running across your roof, climbing down walls, or disappearing into openings on your property is a direct sign of an infestation. Pay attention to where they are going. If you see them repeatedly entering or exiting a specific point on your house, it's likely an entry point they are using to access the interior. Observing their behavior can help pinpoint the areas that need to be sealed.

Unusual Pet Behavior

Your pets, especially dogs and cats, may become agitated or focused on certain areas of your home if they detect the presence of squirrels. They might bark or scratch at walls, ceilings, or floors where squirrels are active. While not a definitive sign on its own, unusual pet behavior combined with other indicators can lend further credence to a squirrel problem.

Fluctuating Temperatures

Damage to insulation by squirrels can lead to noticeable changes in the temperature of your home. If you find that certain rooms are colder in the winter or hotter in the summer than usual, it might be due to compromised insulation in your attic or walls caused by squirrel activity. Improving insulation is often necessary after squirrel removal.

Strange Odors

Beyond the smell of droppings and urine, dead squirrels within walls or attics can produce extremely unpleasant odors as they decompose. If you notice a persistent, foul odor that you can't identify, it could be related to a deceased animal, potentially a squirrel, that couldn't find its way out after entering your property. This requires prompt attention for both odor removal and prevention of further issues.

Presence of Other Pests Attracted by Squirrels

Squirrel nests can sometimes attract other pests. For example, squirrels can carry fleas, ticks, or mites into your home. If you suddenly notice an increase in these other pests, it might be indirectly related to a squirrel infestation. Addressing the squirrel problem helps to eliminate the conditions that are attracting these secondary pests.

Damage to Gardens or Outdoor Structures

While our focus is often on squirrels inside the home, their outdoor activities can also be problematic. If you notice significant damage to your garden, including eaten fruits, vegetables, or flowers, or if you see them burying nuts in your lawn or planters, it indicates that squirrels are active on your property and are likely looking for access points into your home for shelter, especially as weather changes.

Noise in Your Chimney

Squirrels sometimes access homes through chimneys, especially if they are uncapped. You might hear scratching or chattering within your chimney. This can be a dangerous situation, as squirrels can get trapped or even fall into your fireplace. Ensuring your chimney is properly capped is an important preventive measure.

Increased Number of Squirrels on Your Property

If you suddenly seem to have a lot more squirrels running around your yard than usual, it could mean they are considering your property as a potential habitat or a route to a nesting site. While a few squirrels are normal in many areas, a noticeable increase in their population around your home might signal an impending problem.

Damage to Bird Feeders or Outdoor Food Sources

Squirrels are notorious for raiding bird feeders and other outdoor food sources. If your bird feeders are constantly being emptied or damaged by squirrels, it indicates their strong presence and desire for food on your property, increasing the likelihood of them seeking indoor shelter.

If you are experiencing any of these signs, don't wait for the problem to get worse. Squirrel infestations can lead to costly repairs and potential health hazards. Contact Paramount Pest Pros today for professional squirrel removal services in Paramount, CA.

Understanding Squirrel Behavior and Why They Invade Your Home

Understanding why squirrels behave the way they do can help you prevent future issues. Squirrels are primarily motivated by the search for food, water, and shelter. Your home can provide all three, especially during changes in weather or breeding seasons.

Seeking Shelter

Squirrels often seek shelter in homes during cold weather, extreme heat, or when they are ready to build a nest for their young. Attics, walls, and chimneys provide warmth, protection from predators, and a safe place to raise their families. They are also opportunistic and will take advantage of any easily accessible opening.

Finding Mates and Raising Young

Squirrels have two main breeding seasons: one in late winter/early spring and another in mid-summer. During these periods, female squirrels actively look for safe and secluded places to build nests and raise their kits. Your attic or walls can seem like an ideal location, unfortunately for the homeowner. Once a female has established a nest, removing her and her young requires careful and humane handling.

Accessing Food Sources

While squirrels forage outdoors for nuts, seeds, fruits, and other vegetation, they can be attracted to your property by accessible food sources. This includes bird feeders, pet food left outdoors, and even garbage cans. Having a reliable food source nearby can make your home a more attractive option for shelter.

Wearing Down Teeth

Squirrels' incisors grow continuously throughout their lives. To keep them from growing too long, they constantly chew on hard objects. Unfortunately, this can include wood, wiring, pipes, and other components of your home. This chewing behavior is instinctual and is a major reason why they can cause so much damage.

Natural Curiosity

Squirrels are naturally curious animals and will explore potential new habitats, especially if they detect interesting smells or sounds coming from inside your home. A small opening or gap can be perceived as an invitation to investigate, leading them to discover the comfortable and safe environment your home provides.

By understanding these behaviors, you can take steps to make your property less appealing to squirrels. However, once they have established a presence inside your home, professional intervention is almost always necessary for effective and lasting removal. Preventing Future Squirrel Infestations

Preventing squirrels from entering your Paramount home or business is the best long-term solution. While it may not always be possible to prevent every single squirrel from approaching your property, you can significantly reduce the risk of an infestation by taking some proactive steps. Paramount Pest Pros can also provide professional advice and services to help you pest-proof your home against squirrels and other wildlife.

Seal Entry Points

The most crucial step in preventing squirrels is to identify and seal all potential entry points into your home. This includes checking for gaps in your roof, damaged soffits, loose fascia boards, uncapped chimneys, and openings around vents, pipes, and windows. Even small openings can be exploited by squirrels. Use durable materials like wire mesh, sheet metal, or heavy-gauge screening to seal these areas effectively. Regularly inspect your property for any new damage or potential access points.

Trim Trees and Branches

Trees with branches that hang over or touch your roof can provide squirrels with easy access to your home. Trim back any branches that are close to your house to create a buffer zone. This makes it more difficult for squirrels to jump onto your roof or find ways to enter through upper areas.

Secure Chimney Caps and Vent Covers

Uncapped chimneys are a common entry point for squirrels and other wildlife. Install sturdy caps on all chimneys to prevent access. Ensure that all vents, including dryer vents, attic vents, and plumbing vents, are properly screened with durable mesh that squirrels cannot chew through. Regularly check these screens for any signs of damage.

Protect Your Attic and Crawl Space

Ensure that attic and crawl space doors and hatches are tightly sealed and secured. Rodent-proofing these areas from the inside can provide an extra layer of protection against squirrels and other rodents seeking shelter.

Remove Food Sources

Remove potential food sources that attract squirrels to your property. Use squirrel-proof bird feeders or consider temporarily removing feeders if squirrels are a persistent problem. Store pet food indoors in secure containers. Ensure your garbage cans have tight-fitting lids and are stored in a secure location until pickup. Clean up fallen fruits or nuts from trees on your property.

Consider Exclusion Devices

In some cases, installing exclusion devices, such as one-way doors, can be a humane way to allow squirrels that may already be inside to exit while preventing new ones from entering. These devices are typically temporary and should be used in conjunction with sealing other entry points. Paramount Pest Pros can advise you on appropriate exclusion methods.

Maintain Your Property

Regular maintenance of your home's exterior can help you identify potential problems before they become significant issues. Promptly repair any damaged or deteriorating areas of your roof, siding, or foundation. Keeping your property in good condition makes it less appealing and accessible to squirrels and other pests.

Educate Your Neighbors

Sometimes, squirrel problems in a neighborhood are interconnected. Encourage your neighbors to also take steps to prevent squirrel infestations. A collective effort in the community can help reduce the overall squirrel population seeking shelter in homes.

Consult with Wildlife Control Professionals

If you have a history of squirrel problems or if you are unsure how to effectively squirrel-proof your property, consulting with professional wildlife control experts like Paramount Pest Pros is highly recommended. We can conduct a thorough inspection, identify hidden entry points, and implement effective exclusion strategies tailored to your specific Paramount property.

Taking these preventive measures can significantly reduce the likelihood of a squirrel infestation. However, if you already have squirrels in your home, professional removal is the safest and most effective course of action. Attempting to remove squirrels yourself can be dangerous and may not address the root cause of the problem.

Common Myths About Squirrel Removal

There are many misconceptions about squirrels and how to deal with infestations. Relying on myths can be ineffective and potentially harmful to both the squirrels and your property. Let's debunk some of these common myths about squirrel removal in Paramount, CA.

Myth 1: Squirrels Will Leave on Their Own if You Make Noise

While sudden loud noises might startle a squirrel temporarily, they will not permanently deter them from a comfortable and safe nesting spot, especially if they have young. Squirrels are resilient and will often tolerate minor disturbances if the benefits of being in your home outweigh the annoyance. Consistent, ongoing noise might make them uncomfortable over time, but it's not a reliable or quick solution.

Myth 2: You Can Trap and Release Squirrels Anywhere

Simply trapping a squirrel and releasing it elsewhere might seem like a humane solution, but it's not always legal or effective. Many areas have regulations about wildlife relocation to prevent the spread of diseases or to ensure the animal is released into a suitable habitat. Releasing a squirrel far from its home territory can also disorient and stress the animal, reducing its chances of survival. Paramount Pest Pros follows all regulations for humane removal and potential relocation.

Myth 3: Using Repellents Will Keep Squirrels Away Permanently

There are various squirrel repellents on the market, ranging from commercial sprays to home remedies like mothballs or ammonia. While some of these might have a temporary deterrent effect due to strong odors, squirrels are highly adaptable and will often ignore or become accustomed to repellents, especially if there is a strong motivation like shelter or food. Repellents are not a substitute for sealing entry points.

Myth 4: All Squirrels Carry Rabies

While it's true that any mammal can contract rabies, it is very rare for squirrels to carry the disease. Squirrels are generally low-risk animals when it comes to rabies. However, it's still important to maintain a safe distance from wild animals and never attempt to handle them directly, as they can bite or scratch if they feel threatened. Other health concerns can be associated with squirrel droppings and nesting materials, which is why professional cleanup is important.

Myth 5: Squirrel Problems Are Only Common in Rural Areas

While rural areas may have larger wildlife populations, squirrels are highly adaptable and thrive in urban and suburban environments like Paramount, CA. Parks, gardens, and residential areas provide abundant food sources (trees, plants, bird feeders) and ample opportunities for shelter in homes and other structures. Squirrel infestations are a common problem in many populated areas.

Myth 6: You Can Handle Squirrel Removal Yourself Without Risk

Attempting to remove squirrels yourself can be dangerous. Squirrels can bite or scratch if cornered, and they can also carry fleas, ticks, or mites that can transmit diseases to humans. Accessing areas like attics or crawl spaces can also be hazardous. Professional squirrel removal experts have the proper training, equipment, and safety protocols to handle these situations effectively and minimize risks.

Understanding the realities of squirrel behavior and effective control methods is essential for a successful and lasting solution. If you have squirrels in your Paramount home, trust the professionals at Paramount Pest Pros to provide safe and reliable removal services.
